Ingredients

  • Bread: Choose thick slices of bread such as brioche, challah, or French bread. Stale bread works best as it absorbs the egg mixture without becoming too soggy.
  • Eggs: Use large eggs as the base for the French toast batter. The number of eggs depends on the desired serving size.
  • Milk or Cream: Adding milk or cream to the egg mixture helps create a rich and creamy texture. Use about 1/4 to 1/2 cup of milk or cream per 2-3 eggs.
  • Vanilla Extract: Enhance the flavor of your French toast with a splash of vanilla extract. Use about 1 teaspoon for every 2-3 eggs.
  • Cinnamon: Sprinkle in a pinch of ground cinnamon to add warmth and aroma to the French toast. Use about 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon per 2-3 eggs, or adjust to your taste preference.
  • Salt: Add a small pinch of salt to balance the sweetness and enhance the overall flavor.

Steps

  1. In a shallow bowl or pie dish, whisk together the eggs, milk or cream, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt until well combined. This mixture will be the batter for dipping the bread.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of butter or cooking spray to prevent sticking.
  3. Take a slice of bread and dip it into the egg mixture, allowing both sides to soak for a few seconds until well coated.
  4. Place the coated bread onto the preheated skillet or griddle. Repeat this step with additional slices of bread, making sure not to overcrowd the cooking surface.
  5. Cook the French toast for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Adjust the heat if needed to prevent burning.
  6. Transfer the cooked French toast to a plate and continue cooking the remaining slices.
  7. Serve the French toast warm with your choice of toppings. Traditional options include maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh berries, or a dollop of whipped cream. Feel free to get creative with your favorite toppings.
  8. Enjoy your homemade French toast!
