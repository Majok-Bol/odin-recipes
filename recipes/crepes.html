Basic Crepes

Description

Whip up a batch of light and delicate egg-white crepes with our simple recipe. These versatile treats can be enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, or even as a delectable dessert. Using only egg whites, you'll create a healthier twist on the classic crepe while still achieving a delightful texture and flavor. With just a few staple ingredients, you'll be on your way to creating a stack of these paper-thin delights. Fill them with your favorite sweet or savory fillings, from fresh fruits and whipped cream to savory meats and cheeses. Our step-by-step instructions will guide you through the process, ensuring your egg-white crepes turn out perfectly every time. Impress your family and friends with this elegant and delicious culinary creation!

Ingredients

  • Egg Whites: Use about 4-6 large egg whites, depending on the desired serving size.
  • All-Purpose Flour: Measure out 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour. It helps provide structure to the crepes.
  • Milk: Use 1/2 cup of milk. You can opt for low-fat or skim milk if desired.
  • Salt: Add a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor of the crepes.
  • Cooking Spray or Butter: Use cooking spray or a small amount of butter to grease the pan for cooking the crepes.

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the egg whites, all-purpose flour, milk, and a pinch of salt. Whisk the ingredients together until you have a smooth batter. If desired, you can also add sugar or vanilla extract at this stage for sweet crepes.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat. Lightly grease the pan with cooking spray or a small amount of butter.
  3. Pour a small amount of the batter into the hot pan, swirling it around to evenly coat the bottom. Use about 1/4 cup of batter for a 6-8 inch crepe.
  4. Cook the crepe for about 1-2 minutes, or until the edges start to lift slightly and the bottom is lightly golden. Use a spatula to carefully flip the crepe and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes.
  5. Transfer the cooked crepe to a plate and continue the process with the remaining batter. Stack the cooked crepes on top of each other as you go, keeping them warm by covering with a clean kitchen towel.
  6. Once all the crepes are cooked, you can fill them with your desired toppings or fillings. Sweet options include fresh fruits, whipped cream, Nutella, or jam, while savory options can include cheese, ham, or sautéed vegetables.
  7. Fold or roll the crepes with your chosen fillings and serve them warm.

Enjoy your delicious and light egg white crepes as a delightful breakfast, brunch, or dessert option!
