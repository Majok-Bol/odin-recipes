Baby Spinach Omelet

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • 1 cup baby spinach leaves (washed and dried)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings/fillings (such as cheese, diced tomatoes, mushrooms, onions, etc.)

Steps

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, crack the 2-3 large eggs and whisk them together until well combined. You can season the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper at this stage if desired.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or omelet pan over medium heat. Add the butter or cooking oil and allow it to melt and coat the bottom of the pan evenly.
  3. Once the butter has melted, add the baby spinach leaves to the pan. Sauté the spinach for about 1-2 minutes until it starts to wilt. You can season the spinach with a pinch of salt and pepper if desired.
  4. Pour the whisked eggs over the sautéed spinach, ensuring that the eggs are evenly distributed in the pan.
  5. Let the omelet cook undisturbed for a minute or so, allowing the edges to set.
  6. If using any optional toppings or fillings like cheese, diced tomatoes, mushrooms, onions, etc., now is the time to add them to one half of the omelet.
  7. Using a spatula, gently fold the omelet in half, covering the toppings/fillings. Press down lightly to seal the omelet.
  8. Cook the omelet for another minute or two until the eggs are fully cooked and the toppings/fillings are heated through. You can adjust the cooking time based on how well-done you prefer your eggs.
  9. Carefully slide the omelet onto a plate and serve hot.
  10. You can garnish the omelet with additional fresh baby spinach leaves or any desired herbs before serving.
